# The trend of average GRE scores over The Years

Prior to 2020, average GRE total scores remained remarkably stable, hovering around 302 to 304 points. This trend indicates that under traditional in-person testing conditions, both the composition of test-takers and the exam structure were largely consistent.

However, following the outbreak of COVID-19 in 2020, ETS introduced the GRE Home Edition, allowing students to take the exam remotely under digital proctoring. This testing environment differs from traditional test centers, as students use personal devices monitored via webcams and screen sharing rather than being supervised on site. The change coincides with a sharp increase in average total scores, which reached 307 to 309 points between 2021 and 2024, representing a significant rise compared with the previous decade of stability.

Some observers have expressed concern that the remote testing format could increase opportunities for cheating. ETS has acknowledged that Home Edition presents new challenges and has implemented multiple measures to prevent misconduct. Nevertheless, the overall trend suggests that changes in testing conditions are at least partly associated with the rise in scores.

These data come from official ETS statistics, covering hundreds of thousands of test-takers annually. The large sample size and standardized collection methods ensure that the overall trend is reliable.
